Take-home challenge

Purpose

The purpose of this challenge is to evaluate your thought process when faced with new problems as well as your ability to implement technical solutions to those problems.

Deliverables

You have one week to submit your response to this challenge.

  • Your response should be in a Jupyter notebook (or similar sharable format) written in Python or R. Written responses can be in the format of your choice; comments or Markdown in your notebook are fine.
  • Make sure you thoroughly document your thought process in comments or similar. How you arrive at your approach is important.
  • Feel free to use any open source libraries or tools in your analysis.

Challenge description

This challenge will consider a database of crime reports. The starting dataset is available here.

Part 1: imputation

The dataset provided above is missing some values, which are represented as ? -- fill these holes with reasonable values using an imputation method of your choice.

Requirements:

  • Document your rationale for selecting your chosen method(s), as well as any other methods you tried (if any).
  • Retain the imputed form of the dataset for use in Part 2.

Part 2: classification

Build a binary classifier to predict whether a given crime report will lead to an arrest, and evaluate your classifier's expected performance via cross-validation.

Requirements:

  • Use your imputed dataset from Part 1, not the original dataset.
  • Quantify your model's performance using appropriate metrics, and summarize your findings.
  • Document your rationale for selecting your chosen classification method, as well as any other methods you tried (if any).

Part 3: education

In one paragraph or less, describe how your classification method works to a non-technical audience.
